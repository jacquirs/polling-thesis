  • normality tests (shapiro-wilk, jarque-bera) are very sensitive with large n
  • rejecting normality doesn't invalidate clustered standard errors
  • visual inspection of q-q plots more informative than formal tests
  • clustered ses remain valid under non-normality
  • outliers may reflect real campaign events (debates, scandals, etc.)
  • extreme outliers should be investigated but not automatically removed
  • heteroskedasticity handled by clustering, not a major concern
